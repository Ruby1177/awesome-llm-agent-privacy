# Contributing to Awesome LLM Agent Privacy & Compliance Papers

Thank you for your interest in contributing! This list is curated to be a high-quality resource for researchers and practitioners working on privacy, security, and compliance in LLM-based agent systems.

## How to Contribute

### Adding a Paper

1. Fork this repository
2. Add your entry in the appropriate section of `readme.md`
3. Follow the format: `| [Paper Title](arxiv_link) | Authors | Venue Year | Brief description |`
4. Submit a Pull Request with a clear description of the paper and why it fits

### Entry Requirements

- The paper must be **directly relevant** to LLM agent privacy, security, compliance, or related benchmarks
- Prefer peer-reviewed papers (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, USENIX, CCS, S&P, etc.) or highly-cited arXiv preprints
- Include the arXiv link whenever available
- Do not add papers that are primarily about general (non-agent) LLM safety without a clear agent component

### Format

Each entry should follow this format:

```
| [Title](link) | Author1, Author2 | Venue Year | One-sentence description. |
```

### Sections

Place your entry in the most appropriate section:

- **Surveys & Overviews** – broad surveys of LLM agent privacy/security landscape
- **Benchmarks & Measurement** – datasets, benchmarks, evaluation frameworks
- **Attacks & Threat Models** – prompt injection, data extraction, adversarial attacks
- **Defenses & Mitigations** – guardrails, filtering, privacy-preserving techniques
- **Domain-Specific Privacy** – healthcare, finance, legal, corporate settings
- **Compliance, Regulation & Governance** – GDPR, HIPAA, AI Act alignment
- **Infrastructure & Protocols** – MCP, A2A, inter-agent communication security

## Code of Conduct

Be respectful and constructive. We welcome papers from all research groups and institutions.

## Questions?

Open an issue or reach out via the paper page: [AgentLeak on arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11510)
